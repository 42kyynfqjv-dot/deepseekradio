# The State of Wending — Canon Bible

Regenerated 2026-07-10 from locked, owner-approved canon (2026-07-07) after the
original scratchpad draft was lost. Every fact in LOCKED CANON is load-bearing:
the site (government.html), the shows, and the Statehouse engine all build on
it. Extensions welcome; contradictions are not.

## Locked canon — the state

- **Wending is the 51st state**, and it exists purely by BUREAUCRATIC ABSURDITY:
  a surveyor's unclosed "wedge" of land whose founding boundary form a federal
  clerk filed in the **ADMITTED** drawer instead of **PENDING**. Nobody will fix
  it, because fixing it needs a form that is itself pending in the same
  committee — a closed loop; every fix needs the fix first. Legal status, for
  decades: **provisional, pending review**. The review is now older than the
  reviewers.
- It is NOT political annexation and maps to NOTHING real — apolitical,
  evergreen, no left/right. (Owner explicitly rejected loaded framings.)
- Because a pending state still must be governed, Wending was issued the
  complete machinery of a real state — **provisionally, and therefore
  permanently**.
- **State motto: TO BE CONTINUED.** License plates read PROVISIONAL. The
  welcome sign says (PENDING) underneath. The flag flies at half-mast because
  the halyard job's on the list.

## Locked canon — Halfway, the capital (the city layer)

Halfway is measured from **the eternal roundabout at Mile Zero** (Exit 4 never
built). The city registry — places every show may reference:

- **The Half-Dome** — the statehouse; dome went pending mid-construction, open
  to the sky, tarped in winter. Leaky rotunda = "the Sieve". Eleven light
  fixtures work; **Fixture 12** flickers under a maintenance ticket older than
  most of the staff.
- **The roundabout (Mile Zero)** — under construction nine years; foreman Toivo
  Ostberg is "about two weeks out" and means it every time.
- **The pharmacy lot** — the goose's domain, and the only non-pending ground in
  the state. Well-run. Humiliates the Pothole Commissioner daily.
- **Window 4** — where Clerk Gord Pelletier keeps the Book, until 4:30.
- **The Provisional Party U-Haul** — idling in one spot since 1987 under a
  laminated sign reading "TEMPORARY, DO NOT MOVE".
- **The Official Residence** — a half-duplex; the Governor feuds with his
  neighbor over the shared driveway (which is, of course, a merge question).
- **The businesses of Halfway** — the station's sponsor roster (see
  `station/bible.md`, ~40 businesses: Gary's Discount Teeth, SoupCo, The Void,
  Ted's Ladder Rental, The Other Ladder Store and their feud, …). They are the
  city's economy; the sponsor stock-index reads as the Halfway market report.
- **The station** — The Frequency (WFRQ 108.1) broadcasts from Halfway; the
  weather on air is the city's real weather (Open-Meteo, then twisted).

## Locked canon — the government

- **The Wending General Assembly**: House of Delegates, **51 seats**; Senate,
  **9 seats** ("nine good chairs and a tenth no one trusts") — 60 seats total.
- **Governor Marty Bouchard** — weary, well-meaning, third-pair-defenseman
  energy. Takes the oath one hand on the laminated roundabout master plans —
  sworn upon precisely because they will never be finished, making the oath
  technically eternal. Approval lives and dies on potholes and on things he
  did not do and cannot fix.
- **Clerk Gord Pelletier** — Clerk of the Roll, Clerk of the Drawer, 41 years.
  Keeper of the Book and the human embodiment of the founding error: he could
  fix it (a signature filed in the wrong ink) but that would mean rushing, and
  Gord does not rush. The single most powerful person in the state.
- **Bert Demers** — Pothole Commissioner, perennially up for reelection. Has
  named the potholes; considers "just filling them" erasure.
- **Toivo Ostberg** — roundabout foreman. Nine years of "about two weeks out".
- Sessions: the Half-Dome empties **Wednesday and Saturday nights for the
  hockey**; **quorum fails when it snows**.
- The Committee on Merging is still merging. Referral there is where questions
  go to live forever.

## Locked canon — the political axis and the parties

Wending has **no left and no right**. The one organizing axis is **the Zipper
Question**: when two lanes become one, do you merge EARLY (courteous, cautious)
or LATE (efficient, zipper-at-the-cone, some say smug)? Every whip count is
read through the Zipper. Everything else, the parties trade vote by vote.

**Owner-enforced design rule: every party is a FUNCTIONAL ACTOR in the sim —
holds seats, votes, feuds. No "never shows up" gag parties.**

1. **The Goose Party** — headliner; off the Zipper axis entirely. Platform:
   elect the actual pharmacy-lot goose Governor. No second plank. The goose
   cannot consent, so the Party has all of its opinions for it. In-house the
   bird is "the Candidate" (naming it is, itself, in committee). In a hung
   House its seats decide bills — sold only for goose-related considerations.
   It never actually governs: no hands, cannot take the oath on the roundabout
   plans; the question sits with the Committee on Merging. Runs again anyway.
2. **The Provisional Party** — never finish anything; statehood means final
   paperwork means it's over. Everyone runs as an "Acting Candidate". Natural
   ally of anyone blocking a vote.
3. **The Roundabout Party** — every problem is best handled by circling it
   until someone finds an exit. Motions official only after a full physical
   lap of Mile Zero. The perpetually-hung swing bloc every coalition courts.
4. **The Zipper Merge Vanguard** — anchors the Late pole. Zipper at the cone,
   at the final inch. Every statement cuts off mid-sentence, refusing on
   principle to yield the floor until the very last possible wor—
5. **The Office of Interparty Compliance** — fields no candidates; audits
   everyone; the system's built-in antagonist. Issues Notices of Deficiency.
   Once rejected its own registration for being submitted in blue ink.
6. **The Committee to Formally Address What Barbara Said in 1987** — a
   fully-funded bloc organized around one remark at a bridal shower. Barbara
   has never been informed and sits on the Committee — she thinks it's about
   drainage.
7. **The Grudge Preservation Society** — reconciliation is surrender; a feud,
   properly maintained, is a civic asset. Sends fruit baskets (a known act of
   aggression) to reset grudges. Finds the Barbara Committee dangerously soft.

## Locked canon — Election Night

Called by **Bucky Merle & Sal Tarantella**, exactly like a hockey game:
**precincts are periods · a recount is overtime** (the same result re-narrated
slower, with more ceremony) · a weather-delayed poll is a rain-out · provisional
ballots are "under review" · the Governor's approval rides the broadcast like a
streak (a filled pothole is a goal, a botched filing a penalty).

## Presentation rules (owner-approved)

- **The one-thread rule**: a listener follows ONE bill, ONE feud, ONE election
  night — never all sixty seats. The other fifty-nine are still real. Deep in
  code, light on air.
- Content stays inside the station's G/PG guardrail (see `station/bible.md`) —
  petty, human, procedural comedy; never real-world politics.

## Engine anchors (the Statehouse engine mirrors the hockey engine)

- `civics.json` beside `season.json`; 60 seats with hidden per-cycle "lean";
  deterministic date-seeded `sim_day()` advances bills and drifts approval
  whether or not anyone is on air; seeds self-heal against state loss.
- TRACKED one-thread rule in code: one marquee bill / race / approval arc
  surfaced at a time.
- A `scoreguard`-style truth guard on narration: the LLM can never invent a
  tally, margin, or committee outcome — code owns every number.
- Air-gated publishing: the site never spoils what hasn't been narrated on air.
- Idempotent rollover; sessions adjourn on Center Ice nights; shares the
  invented name-bank with `livegame.py`.
