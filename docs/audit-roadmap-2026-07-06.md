## 2. TOP 5 UNCANNY-VALLEY IMPROVEMENTS (impact per dollar per effort)

**I1. One-pipe broadcast chain + per-line leveling + reserve norm — $0/mo, ~half a day.**
At the single choke point (player.sh:45) insert `-af "highpass=f=75,acompressor=threshold=-21dB:ratio=2.5:attack=15:release=250:knee=6:makeup=5,deesser=i=0.3,dynaudnorm=f=400:g=17:p=0.85,alimiter=limit=0.85:attack=4:release=80:level=false"` — targets ~-16 LUFS from today's measured -23.4 dB RMS / -2.3 dB peaks. Decision on the panel contradiction: **dynaudnorm+alimiter, NOT loudnorm** on the live encoder (loudnorm resamples internally to 192 kHz; this box is at load 2.3 on 2 vCPU). In tts.py after `kokoro.create` (line 58), normalize each line to -20 dBFS RMS with a -3 dB peak guard so np.clip at line 70 never fires (peaks at -2.3 dB mean hot lines are being flat-topped today). One-time offline `loudnorm=I=-18:TP=-2:LRA=7` over `/opt/kaos/reserve/*.wav` + filler.wav. Compounding: homogeneous input → the compressor barely works → reserve pieces exit the same pipe at the same loudness. Cheapest station-wide "sounds produced" win; closes the confirmed loudness bug.

**I2. Caller lifecycle + host restraint + absurdity budget + lore rationing — $0/mo (≈+$0.10 prompt tokens), a day of prompt work.**
The loudest content tell: Doreen on the line ~54 minutes across 25 segments, spork-court escalating to gummy-worm soul conga. Four edits, one PR: (a) in `_context` (orchestrator.py:133-139), when `i % parts == 0` inject "SCENE BREAK — previous caller hung up, GONE; open on a NEW caller, new ordinary name" and thread a used-names blacklist through `run_show`; (b) performer rules: call segments are duets (caller ≥40% of lines), host max 2 consecutive lines / ~25 words, caller escalates, host de-escalates and never invents impossible facts (Vivian did 23 consecutive lines and out-weirded her own callers, directly violating personas/vivian.md); (c) absurdity budget: exactly ONE impossible thing per beat, one exchange in three is plain radio (hour/tea/weather), plus a per-beat `grounding` field in the outline schema; (d) lore rationing: performers.py:62 becomes "ignore lore unless the beat asks for a callback", writer picks ≤2 callback beats, cap 2 new jokes per show in lore.py — Gregory the owl aired 22x and the spoon-gavel 28x in 3.5h. Also fold in the confirmed "never explain/praise the bit" bug: ban defining recurring bits and any comment on the show's quality ("You've really built a world here" must never air again).

**I3. Telephone-band callers + voice blending + per-show EQ — $0/mo, hours.**
Tag every non-cast line `phone: true` at the caller branch (performers.py:148-150); in `synth_segment` run tagged lines through a 300-3400 Hz biquad + mild soft-saturation + -3 dB (numpy/scipy, >100x realtime). Host stays full-band: instant host/caller sonic contrast that no same-engine TTS station has, and the bandpass masks Kokoro artifacts on the majority of lines. Same machinery, two bonuses: derive caller voices as deterministic style-vector blends (0.7A+0.3B) to de-duplicate the 15-voice pool and the cast collisions (cosima/dawn/peach all ship af_sarah; kai/wesley share am_michael), and give The Watcher the shortwave treatment (highpass 300/lowpass 3300 + 4:1 comp) — his lo-fi paranoia brand hides TTS weakness entirely. Compounds directly with I2: many short varied callers only lands if callers *sound* like callers.

**I4. Room tone + edge fades, then ducked music beds — $0/mo (CC0/self-synthesized, ~3-5% of a core), hours + a day.**
Ship in two stages. Stage 1 (hours): replace `np.zeros` inter-line gaps (tts.py:67) and the 1s fallback (tts.py:69) with brown-noise room tone at ~-58 dBFS, lay a continuous -63 dBFS floor under each segment, raised-cosine fade-in/out per segment plus 5 ms per-chunk fades, and pre-render a 2.5s roomtone PCM replacing `dd if=/dev/zero` (closes bug #9's spacer half); shorten next-speaker gaps to ~0.3s after `?` lines while in the file. Digital-black silence is the single most reliable "file playback" tell. Stage 2 (day+): per-show beds keyed on segment filename in `feed()` (filenames already encode the show), looped and ducked under the voice with `sidechaincompress`, beds synthesized once on-box or CC0. Beds give the voice an acoustic world, mask the 12 kHz Kokoro ceiling, and paper over chained-part joins. Order matters: I1's chain must be live first so beds get leveled through the same pipe.

**I5. Script-doctor pass — ~+$5/mo (new total ≈ $11/mo), hours.**
`polish_lines()` after `_parse_lines`: one mistral-small-24b call per part (already a configured fallback, config.yaml:23) returning the same JSON schema, editing mechanically only — delete narrated sound effects and precise clock times, merge host runs >2 lines, cut any second impossible thing, cut mid-show greetings, normalize speaker labels to the cast+caller allowlist. This is the deterministic backstop for I2's prompt rules and the sanitizer in bug #1 — it catches the whole class of temp-0.9 mechanical tells (~$0.16/day at ~700 parts/day) for a tenth of the cost of a performer-model upgrade. Add-on while credentials are out: one-time premium cloud re-render of the reserve bumpers/liners, <$1 one-time — the most-repeated audio on the station.

Deployment sequence: I1 → bugs 1-5 → I2 → I3 → I4 stage 1 → I5 → I4 stage 2. Audio fixes carry zero LLM regression risk, so they ship first.

## 3. DO-NOT-DO LIST

1. **TTS engine swap** (Chatterbox/Orpheus/Dia/CSM, or "faster" Piper/Kitten): quality models run 0.1-0.5x realtime and would OOM a box with 371 MB free and no swap; faster models are worse than Kokoro. The 1.47x realtime margin is itself an anti-uncanny asset — buffer starvation mid-scene is the worst artifact possible.
2. **Spot/serverless GPU batch re-rendering**: content is generated 45 min ahead and airs once; there is nothing durable to re-render except the reserve pool, which the <$1 one-time cloud render in I5 covers. $18-30/mo + preemption + a network dependency on a deliberately-local resilience design.
3. **Cloud TTS for the hourly news anchor** (panel 3's headline item): honest math kills it — 24 bulletins/day ≈ 1M chars/mo ≈ $15-16/mo on Polly/Azure, the entire budget on one voice. The Google free tier fits with zero headroom and adds a hard API dependency to the station's signature hourly segment. Revisit only if bulletins drop to 6-8/day or the budget grows.
4. **`loudnorm` on the live encoder** (suggested in the confirmed-bug fix text): single-pass loudnorm internally resamples to 192 kHz — meaningful CPU on this box. Use it only for the offline reserve-pool pass; the live chain is dynaudnorm+alimiter (I1).
5. **48 kHz joint-stereo 128k upgrade**: defer, don't delete. +33% bandwidth per listener, a silent trap in `src/buffer.py` byte math (buffer target would halve), touches every decode path — and buys nothing until stereo beds (I4 stage 2) exist. Revisit after beds are live.
6. **Convolution reverb (afir) / rubberband pitch-shifting on the 24/7 output**: continuous CPU for marginal or negative gain; `aecho` fake early reflections at ~1% CPU covers the per-show space need.
7. **Upgrading the performer model to spend the budget**: the transcript's tells are structural (caller lifecycle, absurdity ratchet, restart amnesia) and mechanical (stage directions, labels) — I2's prompts plus I5's $5 editor fix them; a bigger model at temp 0.9 would reproduce them at higher cost.
8. **More prompt-only enforcement without code backstops**: three confirmed bugs (intro/outro ban, stage-direction ban, name freshness) prove instructions alone lose at temp 0.9. Every content rule in this roadmap ships with a code-level filter or a script-doctor rule.
