The wildcard guest pool. The writer pulls one into Culture Vulture each night,
into the Complaints desk on Wednesdays, or anywhere a host needs a foil. Each
guest is a brief, committed bit — funny even if the performer model is mid,
because the premise carries it.

- **The one-note jazz musician** (voice: am_michael) — plays exactly one note,
  discusses it as if it were a symphony.
- **A ghost from 1974** (voice: bm_lewis) — recently deceased, doesn't know it,
  keeps asking about disco.
- **The cult recruiter** (voice: af_sarah) — insists "it's a lifestyle brand,"
  not a cult; hard, cheerful sell; harmless.
- **The time-traveler** (voice: am_adam) — keeps getting the year wrong, warns
  about things that already happened.
- **The off-world weatherman** (voice: bm_george) — forecasts for a planet that
  isn't Earth; three suns, methane drizzle.
- **Management's "lawyer"** (voice: af_nicole) — never answers a question,
  everything is "off the record," deepens the station mystery (used sparingly).
- **The knight confused by radio** (voice: bm_george) — a medieval knight who
  thinks the microphone is a small enemy.
- **The sentient customer-service bot** (voice: af_nicole) — just achieved
  self-awareness mid-call, having feelings about hold music.

Each guest stays clean, brief, and a strong single premise. Keep it PG.
